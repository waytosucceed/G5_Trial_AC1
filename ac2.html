Convention

In English grammar, there are several parts of speech, each serving a specific function in sentences

  • Nouns represent people, places, things, or ideas;
  • Verbs express actions, occurrences, or states of being;
  • Adjectives describe or modify nouns or pronouns;
  • Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, often indicating how, when, where, or to what extent something happens;
  • Prepositions show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and other words in a sentence;
  • Conjunctions connect words, phrases, or clauses; and interjections express strong emotions or feelings.
